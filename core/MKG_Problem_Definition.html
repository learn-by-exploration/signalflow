Problem Definition

Markets are a network.
No one is treating them as one.

Institutional investors spend $400K–$900K per year per desk on Bloomberg, FactSet, and AlphaSense — and still cannot answer the most important question in finance: when something happens to Company X, which companies are affected, by how much, and why?

📊
What Exists Today
Individual Entity Intelligence
Bloomberg, FactSet, LSEG, and AlphaSense provide deep real-time data, news, and analytics on individual companies — prices, fundamentals, sentiment, filings, earnings transcripts.
🕳️
What Is Missing
Relationship Intelligence
No commercial platform models how companies are connected, how those connections change over time, or how a shock to one entity propagates through its network of dependencies to affect others.
⏱️
The Cost of the Gap
Delayed Second-Order Reactions
Second and third-order effects — supply chain contagion, leadership network exposure, macro sensitivity chains — are discovered manually, hours or days after the market has already moved.
Core Problem Statement
The market prices individual entity news quickly. It prices relationship-driven second and third-order effects slowly. That lag is the alpha opportunity — and it is invisible without a relationship graph.
Capital IQ has a static executive network. AlphaSense has documents. Bloomberg has prices. None of them have a dynamic, weighted, queryable graph of 5,000+ global market entities with a neural network that adjusts relationship strengths in real time from incoming news signals.

The Four Core Problems

What the market fails to provide

01
STATIC RELATIONSHIPS
Every existing system treats company relationships as binary and unchanging. A supply chain link between TSMC and NVIDIA exists or it doesn't. But in reality, that link's strength changes every time TSMC reports earnings, every time geopolitical tension rises, every time a new fab is announced.
NO DYNAMIC WEIGHTS
02
NO PROPAGATION ENGINE
Capital IQ shows you that Person A sits on Board B. But it doesn't tell you: given that news event N just hit Company C, traverse the graph and score every entity that is reachable within 3 hops, weighted by edge strength and time decay.
NO SIGNAL PROPAGATION
03
NO TEMPORAL MEMORY
No platform lets you ask: "What did the relationship graph look like the day before SVB collapsed? Which edges were strengthening? Which were anomalously quiet?" Without temporal graph state, backtesting is impossible to do correctly.
NO TIME-TRAVEL
04
BLACK-BOX OR MANUAL
Quant signals exist (momentum, factor models) but have no explainability. Human research exists but cannot scale to 5,000+ companies globally. There is no middle ground: a scalable, automated system that still shows its full reasoning chain per output.
NO EXPLAINABILITY

The MKG Solution — Architecture Preview

Four layers that solve the problem end-to-end

LAYER 01
Intelligence Agent
Hybrid scraping agent. Continuous for breaking news, scheduled for regional press. Multilingual NLP pipeline. Entity extraction → event classification → impact hypothesis.
LAYER 02
Knowledge Graph
Versioned heterogeneous graph. 6 node types, 7 edge types, multi-relational. Two-speed versioning: slow structural + fast weight time-series with full history.
LAYER 03
Weight Adjustment Network
GAT-based neural network. Trained on historical events first. Adjusts edge weights from incoming signals. Multi-horizon output: 1h / 1d / 1w / macro. Backtest-gated before live.
LAYER 04
Output & Debug Layer
Dashboard + push alerts + REST/WebSocket API. Full signal explainability. Temporal replay UI. Model health monitor. Complete audit trail per signal.
