01 Market Landscape — Size & Segments

The financial data and analytics market spans multiple overlapping segments. MKG operates at the intersection of three of them: the traditional financial data infrastructure (Bloomberg, FactSet), the AI market intelligence layer (AlphaSense, RavenPack), and the fast-growing knowledge graph technology vertical. Each segment is large, each is growing, and none has solved the problem MKG addresses.

The alternative data market — the segment most relevant to MKG's signal output — is the fastest-growing segment in financial services. From $11.65B in 2024, it is projected to reach $135B by 2030 at a 63% CAGR, driven almost entirely by hedge funds seeking alpha signals that traditional data cannot provide. Hedge fund operators account for 68–71% of all alternative data spending.

"More than 60% of buy-side respondents currently use alternative data; 54% use it extensively. The average large fund subscribes to 43 separate datasets, spending $1.6M per year."

The knowledge graph market itself — MKG's core architectural technology — is growing at 37% CAGR and financial services (BFSI) is already its largest vertical. By 2025, 60% of financial institutions are expected to adopt graph analytics as their core fraud detection technology — a proof point that the graph paradigm has institutional legitimacy, even if no one has yet applied it to alpha generation with neural-network-adjusted weights.

06 The Gaps — What the Market Consistently Fails to Provide

These are not minor omissions — they are structural blind spots shared by every commercial platform. Each gap has remained unfilled despite hundreds of millions of dollars in R&D across the industry. The reason is not capability but focus: incumbents optimise for the use cases that generated their initial revenue, not for the new use case that their data structure cannot serve.

01
Dynamic, Neural-Network-Adjusted Edge Weights
Every platform treats relationships as binary or static. Capital IQ shows you the TSMC–NVIDIA supply chain relationship. It does not tell you that the weight of that relationship — the strength of the dependency risk — changed the moment TSMC reported a 12% earnings miss. No commercial system continuously updates relationship strength from news signals through a neural network.
CURRENT STATE: Relationships are database entries. Either connected or not. Weight is implicit and static.
MKG SOLUTION: Weight Adjustment Network (WAN) — reads signals, outputs weight deltas, continuously updates the graph in real time.
All Tier 1 All Tier 2 ~FinDKG (academic only)
02
Automated Multi-Hop Contagion with Quantified Output
AlphaSense finds documents about connected companies. That is manual and reactive. No system takes a news event and automatically traverses a weighted graph to score every reachable entity — with impact score (0–100), direction (±), confidence, and time decay curve — in under 60 seconds. This automated propagation is the core alpha mechanism, and it does not exist commercially.
CURRENT STATE: Analysts manually research second-order effects. Hours or days after the event.
MKG SOLUTION: Event trigger → graph traversal → ranked impact list for every reachable entity in <60 seconds.
All 9 competitors ~Interos (operational, not investing)
03
Full Temporal Graph State — Rewind to Any Timestamp
Bloomberg stores historical prices. AlphaSense stores historical documents. But no platform stores the full state of a relationship graph — structure and weights — at every point in time, queryable to any millisecond. Without this, backtesting is fundamentally flawed: you cannot replay what the model would have known at historical timestamp T without temporal graph state. This also makes event replay (SVB collapse, COVID crash) impossible at the graph level.
CURRENT STATE: Historical data exists for prices and documents. No temporal graph state history exists commercially.
MKG SOLUTION: Two-speed versioning — structural migrations log + edge weight time-series append log. Full rewind in <10 seconds.
All Tier 1 All Tier 2 ~Palantir (limited)
04
On-Premise, Air-Gapped Deployment at Reasonable Cost
Bloomberg, LSEG, FactSet, AlphaSense, and RavenPack are all cloud-only. For institutions with data sovereignty requirements — driven by regulation, competitive sensitivity, or security policy — this is disqualifying. Palantir offers on-premise but at $5M–$50M+/year. FinDKG and Ontotext are self-hostable but not purpose-built for investing. The gap: a purpose-built, on-premise relationship intelligence system at institutional but accessible pricing.
CURRENT STATE: Cloud-only (incumbents) or cloud-only (AI platforms). On-prem only at Palantir's price point ($5M+).
MKG SOLUTION: Designed on-premise from day one. Full stack runs within the institution's own infrastructure. No data sovereignty compromise.
Bloomberg AlphaSense RavenPack ~Palantir ($5M+ only)
05
Full Signal Explainability — Source to Output
In a post-MiFID II world, institutional decision-makers cannot act on unexplained signals. AlphaSense provides document citations. Bloomberg provides headlines. But no platform delivers the complete chain for any given output: this signal scored NVDA at −73 because edge E1 (supply_chain, w=0.91) connects TSMC→NVDA, which was triggered by document D123 (published 09:31 UTC), which caused a weight delta of +0.23 via model v2.3.1 — click each element to see its source. Without this, compliance teams cannot audit, and PMs cannot trust.
CURRENT STATE: Document citations at best. Black-box scores at worst. No complete causal chain from signal to source document.
MKG SOLUTION: Signal tracer — every output links back through: edges → weight deltas → news events → source documents. Fully clickable in dashboard.
All quantitative platforms ~AlphaSense (doc-level only)
06
Autonomous Multilingual Scraping Agent for Global Press
Bloomberg and LSEG have multilingual news but it is curated, expensive, and locked behind their proprietary interfaces. AlphaSense is predominantly English. RavenPack covers 13 languages but via licensed vendor feeds, not autonomous scraping. No platform autonomously and continuously scrapes, translates, and structures news from global regional newspapers (Nikkei, Caixin, Le Monde, Frankfurter Allgemeine, El País) into a proprietary knowledge graph in real time. This proprietary corpus is itself a data moat — competitors cannot replicate it without the same time investment.
CURRENT STATE: Expensive licensed news feeds (Bloomberg, Reuters) OR English-only coverage (most AI platforms).
MKG SOLUTION: Hybrid scraping agent — continuous for breaking news, scheduled for regional press. 6+ languages. On-prem translation. Growing proprietary corpus.
AlphaSense FactSet ~RavenPack (licensed, not scraped)
07
Purpose-Built for Alpha Generation — Not Compliance or Search
The closest graph systems (Capital IQ networks, Interos, Ontotext) are built for compliance, AML, and sanctions screening. The closest signal systems (AlphaSense, RavenPack) are built around document search and sentiment scoring. There is no system built from the ground up with alpha generation as its singular objective — with every design decision (output schema, backtest gate, portfolio integration, multi-horizon architecture) serving that purpose. This is the meta-gap: not a missing feature, but a missing product category.
CURRENT STATE: Graph tools built for compliance. Signal tools built for research. Alpha generation is a secondary use case of tools built for something else.
MKG SOLUTION: Every design decision serves alpha generation. The product exists for this purpose. It is not a repurposed compliance tool.
Capital IQ (compliance) Interos (operations) ~RavenPack (closest, but no graph)
