❌ What It Does NOT Do — Critical Gaps
🔴
Zero relationship graph. Companies exist as isolated entities in Bloomberg. There is no weighted graph of company-to-company relationships, no dynamic edges, no propagation model. The TSMC→NVIDIA supply chain link is not queryable as a graph edge with a weight.
🔴
No event impact propagation. When major news hits, Bloomberg shows the direct price move and the headline. It does not compute which other companies are affected, in which direction, with what confidence, over what time horizon.
🔴
No temporal relationship memory. No versioned graph state. Cannot answer "what did the company relationship network look like on March 15, 2022?" Historical prices exist; historical relationship graph states do not.
🔴
Cloud-only / proprietary network. All data routes through Bloomberg's private network. No on-premise deployment. Institutions with air-gapped or strict data sovereignty requirements cannot process sensitive internal research through Bloomberg infrastructure.
🔴
Archaic UX — massive learning curve. The amber-on-black interface is virtually unchanged since the 1980s. 6–12 months to reach productivity. Junior analysts spend months memorising function codes instead of doing analysis. Onboarding overhead is a real cost.
🔴
Mandatory 2-year lease lock-in. No flexibility to scale down. No monthly options. Firms that reduce headcount continue paying for unused terminals. Contract renewal is high-pressure and non-negotiable for most firms.
🔴
No signal explainability chain. Bloomberg alerts tell you what happened. They cannot produce a full causal chain: event → relationship edge → weight delta → entity impact score → source document. No audit trail for AI-adjacent institutional signals.

❌ What It Does NOT Do
🔴
No relationship graph. AlphaSense finds documents ABOUT entities. It does not model relationships BETWEEN entities as weighted, dynamic edges.
