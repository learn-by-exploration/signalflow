Section 01  ·  Core Problem
The three-speed market,
and who falls through the gap
Every participant in financial markets lives in one of three temporal zones. The first two are overcrowded. The third is completely unaddressed.
The financial data industry has spent 50 years solving the first two speeds. Bloomberg exists entirely to serve Speed 1 — millisecond price data, real-time news, immediate execution. AlphaSense and RavenPack serve Speed 2 — same-day document search, news analytics, intra-industry context. But Speed 3 — the slow propagation of shocks through relationship networks, supply chains, board interlocks, and JVs — remains completely unserved by any commercial platform.
This is not ignorance. Academic finance has documented this problem since 1968. The delay persists because it is cognitively impossible for humans to solve. No analyst can simultaneously cross-reference every company connected to TSMC by every relationship type, weight each connection by its current strength, and score how the shock travels. That requires a graph. A dynamic, neural-network-weighted one.

Section 02  ·  Academic Evidence
The research is unambiguous
Decades of peer-reviewed finance research confirm the three-speed market structure. The gap is not speculative — it is one of the most documented phenomena in academic finance.
Production & Operations Management · 2024 · Qiu, Xu, Yeung, Zeng
Up to 2 weeks
Stock price crash risk is transmitted from major customers to their suppliers with a delay of up to two weeks. Effect is more pronounced as customer importance increases. Supplier information opacity is the primary cause of the delay — the market does not know the strength of the relationship. Contagion also stronger for customer operational incidents versus financial incidents.
ScienceDirect · January 2025 · Chinese A-Share Study (2016–2021)
Asymmetric
Negative CSR events create asymmetric supply chain contagion: customers are affected more negatively than suppliers. The closeness of supply chain relationships and economic policy uncertainty both amplify the contagion. This asymmetry itself represents an exploitable information advantage for anyone mapping relationship strength with dynamic weights.
Ball & Brown 1968 / 55+ years of PEAD research
Months of drift
Post-Earnings Announcement Drift is the oldest and most persistent capital market anomaly. Prices continue drifting in the direction of earnings surprises for weeks to months. PEAD is stronger for small firms, less-covered stocks, firms announcing during busy earnings periods, and firms with high information opacity — including relationship opacity between companies.
440,000+ Earnings Announcements · 6 Global Markets
3-day window
Peer firms in the same country and industry show significant cumulative abnormal return relationships over a three-day window. Effect is magnified in high-volatility markets and for large-cap announcers. This clearly defines Speed 2's operational window — everything beyond 3 days in cross-industry relationships is Speed 3 territory.
UC San Diego Rady School · 89 Billion After-Hours Quotes · 2025
<100ms
Markets price direct earnings news in under 100 milliseconds. Trading opportunities for anyone with even small delays of a few seconds have virtually vanished. This confirms Speed 1 is completely captured with zero alpha remaining — and also confirms that what MKG exploits is architecturally different: not faster delivery of the same news, but a network traversal no tool currently performs.
DellaVigna & Pollett · Investor Inattention / Friday Earnings
60% delayed
On Fridays, 60% of the total stock price response to earnings news is delayed — versus 40% on other weekdays. Structural inattention affects even direct announcements. For complex, non-obvious second-order relationship effects, structural inattention is far greater and not fixable by more analysts. Limited attention is a permanent market structural feature, not a temporary gap.
Shanghai Lockdown Study · 2022 Data · Asia-Pacific Journal Financial Studies 2025
Tier-1 suppliers
During the 2022 Shanghai lockdown: Tesla, VW, GM, SAIC (the focal firms) showed no statistically significant stock reaction — but their tier-1 global suppliers showed significant systematic abnormal returns. The market correctly identified direct focal-firm impacts as already priced, while second-order supply chain impacts were not. This is the exact dynamic MKG exploits.
McKinsey Global Supply Chain Survey · 2024
30% boards
Only 30% of boards have deep understanding of supply chain risks. Companies reverted from regular risk reporting to ad hoc disruption-response reporting in 2024. If the companies themselves lack systematic supply chain intelligence, market participants have no independent mechanism to map relationship networks without dedicated graph infrastructure.

Section 03  ·  Problem Anatomy
Five layers of the problem,
precisely defined
The problem is not "markets are inefficient." Markets are extremely efficient at pricing what they can see. The problem is a structural information architecture failure — the relationship layer of the market is invisible.
01
Relationships Are Stored as Static Binary Records, Not Living Signals
Capital IQ contains "TSMC is a supplier to NVIDIA." It does not contain "TSMC's supply of 5nm capacity to NVIDIA represents 11% of TSMC's total revenue, constitutes a risk dependency that intensified by ~40% in the past 18 months as NVIDIA's order book expanded, and is projected to reach 63% of TSMC's advanced packaging capacity in 2026." The relationship exists as a binary record. Actual weight, direction, intensity, trend — none of this is tracked.
This is not a data availability problem. The signals to compute these weights exist across earnings calls, news articles, and transaction volumes. No system continuously reads those signals and updates a weighted relationship graph. Every time the underlying relationship changes, the database entry stays the same.
→ Evidence: Nasini & Erdemlioglu model shows stock price dynamics require multi-channel network weights (supply chain, competition, partnerships) with time-varying parameters. No commercial system implements this.
02
Human Analyst Capacity Is the Hard Ceiling — and It Does Not Scale
A skilled semiconductor analyst can track ~30–50 companies and ~200–300 named relationships between them. That is the human ceiling. The global listed equities market contains 58,000+ companies with millions of relationship edges. Even if all buy-side analysts worked simultaneously on relationship signals, total human capacity would cover perhaps 5% of the relevant relationship graph.
This is why TSMC→NVIDIA is well-known — thousands of analysts cover it. But NVIDIA's 800+ enterprise customers, 200+ software partners, and 400+ data center partners are almost entirely uncovered from a relationship-signal perspective. When TSMC's earnings imply something about each of those entities, the information travels through no systematic mechanism to reach those stock prices.
→ Evidence: 87% of buy-side firms use GenAI primarily for document summarization and research — NOT for relationship mapping or network traversal. The bottleneck is not intelligence but architecture. (AIMA, 2024)
03
Signals Are Scattered Across Incompatible Unstructured Sources
Signals for computing dynamic relationship weights exist across: SEC filings (quarterly, structured), earnings call transcripts (semi-structured NL), news articles (continuous, unstructured), press releases (event-driven), analyst reports (sparse), regional press in 6+ languages (mostly unindexed), and social media (noisy). No single system collects all of these, extracts relationship signals, and maps them to entity-pair edges simultaneously.
AlphaSense has 500M+ documents but is fundamentally a search interface — it finds documents about entities, not relationship weight changes between entity pairs. Bloomberg has real-time news but zero graph layer. RavenPack produces entity-level sentiment scores attached to individual entities — not to relationship edges between pairs. The graph layer is entirely absent in every existing platform.
→ Evidence: RavenPack covers 40,000+ sources in 13 languages with 80+ sentiment fields per entity — but those fields are on individual entities, not on the edges between them. The graph architecture does not exist.
04
No Temporal Memory — Every Relationship Is Treated as Eternal Until Changed
When NVIDIA announced it would receive ~63% of TSMC's increased advanced packaging capacity in 2025, this massively changed the risk profile of the TSMC→NVIDIA relationship. No system: (a) detected that signal, (b) updated the edge weight, (c) stored the old weight with a timestamp, or (d) propagated the implication to NVIDIA's downstream customers. The change is invisible in every commercial data platform.
Without temporal memory, backtesting is impossible at the relationship level. "What would the model have known in November 2024 about TSMC's capacity allocation?" is unanswerable by any existing commercial system. This means the predictive validity of relationship signals cannot be proven — which is why the alpha opportunity has gone unexploited at commercial scale despite being documented for 55+ years.
→ Evidence: Post-Earnings Announcement Drift persists partly because investors fail to incorporate implications of current earnings for future earnings (Bernard & Thomas 1990). Without temporal relationship state, that forward inference across the network is computationally impossible.
05
Explainability Is Zero — Signals Without Causal Chains Are Institutionally Unusable
Even if a PM received a signal: "Company X will be negatively impacted by TSMC's earnings in 5 days, confidence 78%," they cannot act on it. Fiduciary decisions require documented rationale. MiFID II requires explainability for AI-driven investment decisions. Risk committees require audit trails. "The model said so" fails every institutional compliance test.
This is the reason quant funds using black-box signals face increasing regulatory pressure. The full causal chain is required: relationship edge E1 (supply_chain, w=0.91) → news event D at T=09:31 UTC → weight delta +0.23 on edge E2 → entity score of −73. Every element clickable, timestamped, model-versioned. Without this, no institutional investor can legally or operationally use the signal.
→ Evidence: 50% of North American hedge fund managers cite regulatory compliance as a top concern for AI tool adoption (Hedgeweek 2024). SEC 2023 rules tightened AI signal disclosure requirements. MiFID II requires documented rationale for AI-assisted investment decisions at institutional scale.

When TSMC announces disruption, 12 pods simultaneously face concentrated risk through relationships they don't know they share. The pod shop model with $342B AUM and $2T gross exposures is structurally exposed.
Tertiary — Thematic / Macro PM
A manager building an AI infrastructure thematic position needs every company benefiting from GPU buildout. Bloomberg finds the obvious ones. But third-ring companies — specialized software vendors, electrical grid operators near data centers, industrial gas suppliers, fiber providers for AI campuses — require a relationship graph traversal no tool provides. The PM's thematic position is always incomplete and always late to less-obvious beneficiaries.
Deep Niche — Quant Signal Developer
A quant team building systematic strategies needs to model which events at which firms propagate to which others with what magnitude and timing. Without a dynamic relationship graph, they estimate this from historical correlations — backward-looking, capturing spurious correlations, failing to reflect current relationship states. The academic proof exists. The commercial tool does not.

Section 05  ·  The Alpha Mechanism
Why this generates
exploitable, durable alpha
The three-speed market structure creates an alpha opportunity that is predictable (academic research has confirmed the delay patterns for 55+ years), exploitable (the mechanism — relationship graph traversal with weighted edges — is implementable with current technology), and durable (the delay exists for structural cognitive reasons, not temporary information gaps).
The structural reason the delay persists: human cognitive limits cannot be removed by faster news delivery. Getting TSMC's earnings faster does not help a PM know which of their 2,000 tracked companies has an undiscovered second-order dependency on TSMC capacity. Even with AlphaSense surfacing every relevant document, the PM must still manually map those documents to relationship weights, traverse the network, and score the impact. That takes hours to days. The market moves while they work.

The Problem — Summary Statement
Markets price direct earnings news in milliseconds. They price intra-industry spillovers in hours. But the shock propagation through supply chains, board networks, joint ventures, and regulatory co-exposures — confirmed by decades of peer-reviewed research to take days to weeks — is completely invisible to every commercial financial data platform.

The reason is structural: relationship networks are stored as static binary records, not as weighted, time-varying graphs. No commercial system continuously reads news signals, extracts relationship implications, updates edge weights, traverses the graph, and scores downstream impact — automatically, in real time, with full explainability.

The alpha opportunity is not a theory. It is a documented market anomaly confirmed since 1968. The technology to exploit it exists. The product does not. That is what MKG is.
